Our Story

A family, a hillside, a Winery

Greppo Propria was planted by Hisham Jerdak in 2016, on a south-facing hillside his own.The Jerdak family still lives on the estate — and still believes that hospitality and winemaking are the same craft, practiced twice a day.

What began as a modest land has become one of Choueir's most quietly celebrated estates: a working vineyard, a Michelin-inspired table, and a home for the kind of gatherings — weddings, harvest dinners, long August afternoons by the pool — that people remember for the rest of their lives.

Hisham Jerdak Founder and creator of Greppo Propria

The Vineyard Tour

Walk the rows with us

South-facing terraces, planted in 2016

South-facing, Mediterranean

Every parcel at Greppo Propria faces south or south-west, catching the full arc of Choueir's and Ain Al Sendianeh's sun. The Mediterranean climate — hot, dry summers, mild winters — is exactly what Sangiovese and its neighboring varieties were bred for.

1.2 metres between vines, by design

Spacing as a decision

Vines are trained 1.2 metres apart in narrow rows, forcing roots to compete and dig deep for the estate's mineral — the single biggest reason our fruit tastes the way it does.

Hand-picked, basket by basket

Harvest, by hand

Every cluster at Greppo Propria is still picked by hand, into 15kg crates, usually before nine in the morning while the fruit is cool. Nothing is mechanically harvested — nothing rushed.

Certified organic since 2009

Organic, on principle

No synthetic pesticides or herbicides have touched these hills since 2009. Cover crops, sheep and hand-hoeing do the work chemistry used to — slower, but it shows in the glass.

The Wine Cellar

Carved into the rock

Our cellar holds constant conditions the family has never needed machinery to achieve: 14°C, 75% humidity, total darkness, all year round.

  • French oak — Allier and Tronçais barriques, for the Riserva
  • Italian oak — Slavonian botti, for longer, gentler aging
  • Barrel room — 400 barrels resting at any given time
  • Bottle library — every vintage since 2016, kept in the deepest room

The Infinity Pool

Where the vines meet the sky

The pool's edge was cut to disappear exactly at the horizon line of the valley — a small piece of engineering that took one very patient stonemason to get right.

  • Sunset views — west-facing, over the lower terraces
  • House wine bar — poolside, open from midday
  • Open — daily, April to October

St Elias

The chapel

A small stone chapel, deconsecrated in the 2016s and gently restored, stands at the most gorgeous point of the estate. Private vows are still exchanged here, with every row of vines visible from the chapel steps.

  • Seats — up to 30 guests
  • Ceremonies — civil, symbolic and non-denominational
  • Included — a private tasting for the couple beforehand
